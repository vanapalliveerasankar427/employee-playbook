Toolkit Career Development & Progression Performance Review Prep Checklist
PERFORMANCE REVIEW PREP

Walk In Confident → Evidence-Based Self-Advocacy

Don't rely on memory or hope your manager remembers your wins. Use this checklist to document achievements, gather evidence, and prepare strategic talking points so you walk into your review ready to advocate for yourself with confidence.
Category
Career Development & Progression

When to Use This Checklist

  • 2–4 weeks before your scheduled performance review
  • When preparing your self-assessment or self-reflection document
  • Before a mid-year check-in or informal performance conversation
  • When requesting a promotion, raise, or role change
  • After completing a major project or milestone (document while fresh)
  • When you want to ensure your contributions are visible and valued

Pro Tips

  • Keep a running "wins doc" throughout the year so prep is easier
  • Use specific numbers, metrics, and outcomes — not vague feelings
  • Prepare examples for each competency or value your company tracks
  • Ask colleagues for feedback 2 weeks before your review
  • Frame everything as impact, not just activity or effort
  • Prepare 2–3 career development goals for the next review period

Your Review Prep Checklist

I've documented my key achievements, wins, and contributions from this review period.
I've gathered specific metrics, data, or measurable outcomes that demonstrate my impact.
I have concrete examples prepared for each competency or value my company assesses.
I've reviewed my goals from the last review period and can articulate progress on each one.
I've gathered feedback from colleagues, stakeholders, or clients to support my self-assessment.
I've identified challenges I faced and how I navigated them (not just what went well).
I've prepared 2–3 development areas or growth goals for the next review period.
I've prepared talking points for my career aspirations and next steps.
I understand what "meets expectations" vs "exceeds expectations" means for my role and level.
I've completed my self-assessment document (if required) with specific, evidence-based responses.
I've prepared questions to ask my manager about feedback, growth opportunities, or next steps.
I'm prepared to receive feedback constructively and advocate for myself with confidence.
