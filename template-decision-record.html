DECISION RECORD TEMPLATE

Capture the decision, the why, and the trade-offs — so it survives scrutiny, politics, and future memory gaps

Move from “we agreed something” to a clean record: context, options, rationale, risks, owners and review points. Protect credibility, reduce rework, and stop decisions being quietly rewritten later.
Related categories
Clarity, Priorities & Direction Workplace Dynamics & Politics Decision Making & Career Path

When to Use This Template

  • After a meeting where “something was agreed” but you want it unambiguous.
  • When decisions keep getting revisited, reframed, or quietly rewritten.
  • Before implementing something that affects scope, resourcing, risk, or reputation.
  • When you’re being asked to “just do it” without clarity on trade-offs.
  • Any time you want a calm paper trail that holds under scrutiny.

How to Use It (fast)

  • Write the decision statement in one sentence (clear enough to quote).
  • List options considered + why they were rejected (avoids hindsight blame).
  • State assumptions explicitly — then add a review trigger if they change.
  • Lock owners, next actions and a review date.
  • Send a recap: “If anything is incorrect, reply with amendments within 24 hours.”

Build your decision record

Four sections: (1) decision basics, (2) context + constraints, (3) options + rationale, (4) risks + alignment + next steps. Generate a clean Decision Record when you have enough to be specific and defendable.
