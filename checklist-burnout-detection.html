BURNOUT DETECTION CHECKLIST

Spot the Warning Signs Before It's Too Late

Burnout doesn't happen overnight. Use this checklist to identify early warning signs, assess your burnout risk level, and create a personalised recovery plan before you hit crisis point.
Category
Expectations, Burnout & Capacity

When to Use This Checklist

  • When you're feeling constantly exhausted despite adequate sleep
  • When work no longer feels meaningful or rewarding
  • When you're increasingly cynical or detached from your work
  • When your performance is declining despite working harder
  • When stress is affecting your physical or mental health
  • As a regular quarterly check-in on your wellbeing

Pro Tips

  • Be honest with yourself — denial delays recovery
  • Track your score over time to spot trends early
  • Share your results with someone you trust for external perspective
  • Don't wait for "permission" to take action — burnout is a serious health risk
  • Recovery takes time — small consistent changes work better than dramatic fixes
  • Professional support (GP, counsellor, coach) can accelerate recovery significantly

Your Burnout Detection Checklist

I feel physically and emotionally drained most of the time, even after rest or holidays.
I struggle to find energy for work, and simple tasks feel overwhelming.
I've become cynical or critical about my work and the people I work with.
I feel detached, numb, or indifferent about work that used to matter to me.
I doubt my abilities and question whether I'm good at my job.
My performance has declined and I'm making more mistakes than usual.
I have trouble concentrating, remembering things, or making decisions.
I'm experiencing physical symptoms (headaches, stomach issues, tension, sleep problems).
I feel irritable, anxious, or depressed more often than usual.
I'm withdrawing from colleagues, friends, or family.
I'm relying more on unhealthy coping mechanisms (alcohol, food, avoiding people).
Work feels meaningless and I can't see a way forward that feels sustainable.
