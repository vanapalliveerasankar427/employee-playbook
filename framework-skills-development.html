SKILLS DEVELOPMENT FRAMEWORK

Build skills that actually get recognised

This is a working framework (not “do a course”). Define the skill, build a 30-day practice plan,
create proof, and generate a clean message you can use with your manager.
Related Categories
Career Playbook

When to Use This Framework

  • When you’re doing “good work” but it’s not translating into progression or visibility.
  • When feedback is vague (“be more strategic”, “show ownership”) and you need a concrete plan.
  • When you’re pivoting roles and need proof you can operate at the next level.
  • When you want a pay rise/promotion case built on evidence, not hope.
  • When you’re under pressure and need growth without burning yourself out.

Pro Tips

  • Define the skill as a behaviour + outcome — not a label (“strategic”).
  • Pick one skill and win. Scatter-learning feels productive but rarely changes outcomes.
  • Build proof in normal work: artefacts, decisions, stakeholder outcomes, risk reductions.
  • Ask for feedback in a way people can answer: “Was this clear? What would you change?”
  • Protect your time: skills grow through a small weekly rhythm, not one heroic week.

Recognition rule

Skills “count” when they show up as observable outcomes: decisions made, risk reduced, stakeholders aligned, quality improved.

Level-up lens

If the target is a higher level, your plan must include ambiguity, trade-offs, and influence — not just harder tasks.

Quick Coverage Checklist

The canvas is the framework. This checklist is a final audit to confirm your plan is specific, measurable, and visible (without burning you out).
I chose one primary skill and defined it as behaviour + outcome (not a vague label).
I set a realistic time horizon and a success signal someone else would recognise.
I captured concrete baseline evidence (artefacts, outcomes, examples) — not just feelings.
I named the real gap and the failure mode (where I break down under pressure).
I created a weekly practice rhythm with a smallest repeatable practice unit.
I included an on-the-job experiment that will generate proof in normal work.
I identified who needs to see the skill and what proof artefacts I’ll produce.
I set a visibility rhythm (updates / check-ins) so progress converts into recognition.
I wrote a clean manager ask that is specific and easy for them to support.
I respected constraints (capacity/time) and avoided a plan that requires constant overtime.
