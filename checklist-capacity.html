CAPACITY CHECKLIST

Facts Over Feelings → Capacity Evidence

When you feel overloaded or at capacity, use this checklist to move from vague overwhelm to clear evidence. You’ll collect practical data about workload, time, and scope — so conversations about priorities and deadlines become simple and factual.
Explore Categories
Evidence-Based Conversations Boundary Setting & Advocacy

When to Use This Checklist

  • When you feel constantly overwhelmed or at capacity
  • Before a workload conversation with your manager
  • When you’re being handed “just one more thing”
  • When deadlines feel unrealistic
  • When your workload doesn’t match your level or role
  • When planning the next quarter or a performance review

Pro Tips

  • Track actual time for 1–2 weeks for the clearest picture
  • Include meetings, email, admin, and context switching
  • Use numbers where possible — they land harder than feelings
  • Use this as a collaboration tool, not a weapon
  • Bring options: what drops, what shifts, what changes
  • Update it whenever your scope changes

Your Capacity Checklist

I've tracked my actual time spent on different types of work for at least one full week.
I know how many hours I'm actually working each week (not just “feels like” a lot).
I've calculated what percentage of my week is meetings versus focused work.
I've accounted for “invisible work” — emails, quick questions, admin tasks, context switching.
I've documented all current responsibilities and projects with estimated time requirements.
I can compare my workload to my job description or role expectations.
I've assessed whether my current workload is sustainable long-term without burnout.
I've identified what percentage of my time is spent on growth vs maintenance work.
I know how much time I actually have for priority work versus reactive work.
I've calculated what buffer I have for unexpected work or emergencies.
I've created a simple visual (chart or summary) of my workload distribution.
I've identified what could be deprioritised, delegated, or redesigned to create capacity.
